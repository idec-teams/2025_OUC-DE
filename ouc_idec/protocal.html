Molecular Biology Protocols

Site-Directed Mutagenesis

Materials

  • PyAly expression vector (pET28a)
  • NNK degenerate primers for target residues
  • Phusion High-Fidelity DNA Polymerase
  • DpnI restriction enzyme
  • E. coli DH5α competent cells
  • LB agar plates with kanamycin (50 μg/mL)

Procedure

  1. Design primers with NNK codons at target positions
  2. Set up PCR reaction: 30 cycles of 98°C for 10s, 55°C for 30s, 72°C for 2 min/kb
  3. Digest template DNA with DpnI at 37°C for 1 hour
  4. Transform into E. coli DH5α and plate on selective media
  5. Screen colonies by colony PCR and sequence verification
Note: Use high-fidelity polymerase to minimize random mutations. Verify all mutants by sequencing before protein expression.

Protein Expression and Purification

Materials

  • E. coli BL21(DE3) competent cells
  • LB medium with kanamycin (50 μg/mL)
  • IPTG (1M stock solution)
  • Lysis buffer (50 mM Tris-HCl, 300 mM NaCl, 10 mM imidazole, pH 8.0)
  • Ni-NTA affinity resin
  • Elution buffer (50 mM Tris-HCl, 300 mM NaCl, 250 mM imidazole, pH 8.0)

Procedure

  1. Transform expression vector into BL21(DE3) cells
  2. Grow culture in LB medium at 37°C until OD600 = 0.6-0.8
  3. Induce with 0.5 mM IPTG and incubate at 18°C for 16 hours
  4. Harvest cells by centrifugation and resuspend in lysis buffer
  5. Lyse cells by sonication and clarify by centrifugation
  6. Purify protein using Ni-NTA affinity chromatography
  7. Dialyze against storage buffer and determine concentration

Biochemical Assays

Alginate Lyase Activity Assay

Materials

  • Sodium alginate substrate (1% w/v in 50 mM Tris-HCl, pH 8.0)
  • Purified alginate lyase mutants
  • Reaction buffer (50 mM Tris-HCl, 100 mM NaCl, pH 8.0)
  • Trichloroacetic acid (10% w/v)
  • UV-Vis spectrophotometer

Procedure

  1. Prepare reaction mixture: 450 μL substrate + 50 μL enzyme
  2. Incubate at 37°C for 30 minutes
  3. Stop reaction by adding 500 μL 10% TCA
  4. Centrifuge at 12,000 × g for 10 minutes
  5. Measure absorbance of supernatant at 235 nm
  6. Calculate activity using extinction coefficient ε = 6150 M⁻¹cm⁻¹

HPLC Analysis of Oligosaccharides

Materials

  • HPLC system with refractive index detector
  • Bio-Gel P-4 column (300 × 7.8 mm)
  • Mobile phase: 0.1 M ammonium acetate, pH 5.2
  • Alginate oligosaccharide standards (DP1-DP6)
  • Enzymatic reaction products

Procedure

  1. Filter samples through 0.22 μm membrane
  2. Set column temperature to 55°C
  3. Use isocratic elution at 0.4 mL/min flow rate
  4. Inject 20 μL sample and run for 60 minutes
  5. Identify peaks using oligosaccharide standards
  6. Calculate tetrasaccharide percentage from peak areas
Note: Calibrate the system regularly with fresh standards. Ensure consistent column temperature for reproducible retention times.

Data Preparation

Model Training

  2. Implement random forest and gradient boosting algorithms
  3. Perform 5-fold cross-validation for hyperparameter tuning
  4. Evaluate models using R² and mean absolute error
  5. Select best model for multiple mutant prediction
