Experimental Design

Rational Design Strategy

Our design strategy focused on modifying the substrate-binding pocket of PyAly to enhance tetrasaccharide production. Based on structural analysis, we identified four key residues (R143, R159, F170, K172) in loop2 region that influence substrate recognition and product distribution.

These residues were selected for saturation mutagenesis to explore their individual and combined effects on tetrasaccharide yield.

Site-Directed Mutagenesis

We employed saturation mutagenesis at four key positions to generate single mutants. Each position was mutated to all 20 natural amino acids to comprehensively explore the mutational space.

The mutants were expressed in E. coli and purified for enzymatic activity assays. Tetrasaccharide content in the degradation products was measured using HPLC analysis.

Machine Learning Integration

Single mutant data were used to train machine learning models for predicting tetrasaccharide yield of multiple-site mutants. We employed random forest and gradient boosting algorithms to identify optimal combinations.

The models considered structural features, physicochemical properties, and sequence conservation to predict the effects of multiple mutations.

Multiple Mutant Design

Based on machine learning predictions, we designed four multiple-site mutants combining favorable mutations from single mutant analysis. These included double and triple mutants predicted to enhance tetrasaccharide production.

The designed mutants were experimentally validated to confirm the predictive accuracy of our models and to identify the most effective combinations.
