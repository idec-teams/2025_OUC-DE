Laboratory Notebook

Project Timeline and Progress

Initial Vector Construction and Mutagenesis
March 15, 2025
Objective: Clone PyAly gene into pET28a vector and generate initial single mutants at positions R143, R159, F170, K172.

Methods

  • Amplified PyAly gene from genomic DNA using specific primers
  • Digested PCR product and pET28a vector with NdeI and XhoI
  • Ligated insert into vector and transformed into E. coli DH5α
  • Verified construct by sequencing
  • Performed site-directed mutagenesis using NNK primers

Results

  • Successfully cloned PyAly into pET28a with correct orientation
  • Generated 12 single mutants covering all target positions
  • Sequencing confirmed correct mutations without unwanted changes

Conclusion

Initial cloning and mutagenesis successful. All mutants ready for protein expression and purification.

Protein Expression Optimization
April 2, 2025
Objective: Optimize expression conditions for PyAly and its mutants in E. coli BL21(DE3).

Methods

  • Transformed expression vectors into BL21(DE3)
  • Tested induction temperatures: 16°C, 18°C, 25°C, 37°C
  • Tested IPTG concentrations: 0.1 mM, 0.5 mM, 1.0 mM
  • Induction times: 4 hours, 8 hours, 16 hours
  • Analyzed expression by SDS-PAGE

Results

  • Optimal conditions: 18°C, 0.5 mM IPTG, 16 hours induction
  • High soluble expression under optimized conditions
  • Some mutants showed reduced expression compared to wild-type
Note: R159N mutant showed particularly good expression levels, suggesting improved protein stability.

Enzyme Activity Screening

Initial Activity Screening of Single Mutants
April 20, 2025
Objective: Screen all single mutants for alginate lyase activity and tetrasaccharide production.

Methods

  • Purified all mutants using Ni-NTA affinity chromatography
  • Standardized protein concentrations to 1 mg/mL
  • Performed activity assays with 1% sodium alginate
  • Analyzed products by HPLC with Bio-Gel P-4 column
  • Quantified tetrasaccharide percentage from chromatograms

Results

  • R159N showed highest tetrasaccharide yield: 46.2%
  • K172E also showed significant improvement: 35.8%
  • Most mutants retained good enzymatic activity
  • Some mutations (F170A) reduced overall activity

Conclusion

R159N identified as the most promising single mutant. Data suitable for machine learning model training.

Machine Learning and Multiple Mutant Design

Machine Learning Model Development
May 10, 2025
Objective: Develop predictive models for tetrasaccharide yield of multiple mutants.

Methods

  • Compiled single mutant data with structural features
  • Trained random forest and gradient boosting models
  • Used 5-fold cross-validation for model evaluation
  • Selected top 4 multiple mutants for experimental validation

Results

  • Random forest model achieved R² = 0.89 on test data
  • Predicted R159N_F170Y_K172E as best triple mutant
  • Models showed good generalization to multiple mutations
Validation of Multiple Mutants
May 25, 2025
Objective: Experimentally validate predicted multiple mutants.

Methods

  • Generated four designed multiple mutants
  • Expressed and purified proteins as before
  • Performed activity assays and HPLC analysis
  • Compared experimental results with predictions

Results

  • R159N_F170Y_K172E achieved 43.9% tetrasaccharide yield
  • All predictions within 1% of experimental values
  • Successful validation of machine learning approach

Conclusion

Machine learning models accurately predicted multiple mutant effects. R159N_F170Y_K172E identified as optimal mutant for tetrasaccharide production.
